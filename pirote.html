Architectural Description – Pirote House, Rwamagana

The Pirote House in Rwamagana is a smart and efficient 2-in-1 residential development, designed as a model for affordable modern housing on a standard 15m x 20m urban

plot in Rwanda. Located in the fast-growing town of Rwamagana, this project reflects a forward-thinking approach to housing by combining compact design, contemporary

aesthetics, and practical spatial organization.

Each of the two units within the structure is fully self-contained, allowing for flexible use—either for multi-generational living, owner-occupier with rental, or dual rental

investment. The overall form is kept simple and compact to optimize land use, leaving room for front-side parking, small garden spaces, and future expansion.

The design embraces a modern open-plan layout, which combines the living, dining, and kitchen areas into one continuous space. This spatial strategy allows for maximum

efficiency, improves natural ventilation and lighting, and creates a feeling of spaciousness despite the modest footprint. Private areas—such as bedrooms and bathrooms—are

zoned toward the rear for added privacy and acoustic comfort.

The architecture features clean, geometric lines and a minimalist form that balances function and visual appeal. The house is constructed with modern, cost-effective materials

selected for both durability and aesthetics:

including solar water heaters and rainwater harvesting systems.

Theo Estate stands as a benchmark for modern suburban housing in Kigali, offering residents a balance of aesthetic value, construction quality, and sustainable living within a well-planned community environment.
